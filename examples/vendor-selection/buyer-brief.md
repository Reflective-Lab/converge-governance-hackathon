# Buyer Brief

## Business Problem

The buyer needs an AI partner strategy for governed business workflows.

The immediate decision is framed as a vendor selection: choose the best candidate from a submitted RFI/RFP response set.

The deeper need is broader: support agentic reasoning, structured synthesis, web evidence, compliance review, auditability, cost control, and policy enforcement without locking the organization into a brittle single-provider answer.

## Upstream Assumptions

1. Management has defined the business problem and why it matters.
2. Procurement or the owning division has assigned an accountable owner.
3. Stakeholders are known: business, IT, security, legal, finance, and likely users.
4. A market scan or RFI has produced the candidate set.
5. The RFP package has been sent, responses have come back, and first-pass criteria exist.
6. The buyer has a budget envelope, timeline, and non-negotiable gates.

## Scope For This Demo

In scope:

- Normalize a structured vendor response pack.
- Detect missing, pending, or contradictory evaluation inputs.
- Compare compliance, cost, risk, capability, and certification coverage.
- Produce a ranked shortlist and evidence-backed synthesis.
- Stop at HITL/Cedar gates when authority or approval is missing.
- Preview when the better answer is a governed provider mix rather than one vendor.

Out of scope for the live demo:

- Running the original RFI/RFP issue process.
- Vendor Q&A, demonstrations, and BAFO negotiation.
- Legal redlining and final contract execution.
- Production onboarding.

## Success Criteria

The output should be clear enough for a business stakeholder to answer:

- What did the system recommend?
- Why did that option win?
- Which candidates were rejected or blocked?
- What evidence and assumptions drove the result?
- Which human or policy gate allowed or stopped the decision?
- What should happen next?
